“Actually make a man a criminal against ’is will,” said the Carpenter.  “’Ere I am, old, younger men takin’ my place, my clothes gettin’ shabbier an’ shabbier, an’ makin’ it ’arder every day to get a job.  I go to the casual ward for a bed.  Must be there by two or three in the afternoon or I won’t get in.  You saw what happened to-day.  What chance does that give me to look for work?  S’pose I do get into the casual ward?  Keep me in all day to-morrow, let me out mornin’ o’ next day.  What then?  The law sez I can’t get in another casual ward that night less’n ten miles distant.  Have to hurry an’ walk to be there in time that day.  What chance does that give me to look for a job?  S’pose I don’t walk.
